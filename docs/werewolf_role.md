# Werewolf Roles — Ultimate Werewolf (Official Manual)

Source: `Ultimate-Werewolf-Manual.txt` (role cards section). Descriptions cleaned
up from the PDF text extraction; alternates are part of each role's card text.

> Note: This manual covers the **Ultimate Werewolf** role set. Some roles in the
> "Ultimate Deluxe Edition" 45-role list (e.g. Beholder, Fruit Brute, Sasquatch,
> Dire Wolf, etc.) are not described in this manual; conversely, several roles
> here (Masons, Mayor, Priest, Old Hag, etc.) are not in the Deluxe 45. Tag any
> uncertain rule with `ponytail:` for review during encoding.

---

## Apprentice Seer
The Apprentice Seer is woken up when the Seer is called, after the Moderator has
indicated to the Apprentice Seer that the Seer is eliminated (by a tap on the
shoulder during the calling of the Seer at night, if roles are not revealed).
The Apprentice Seer then takes over the Seer's power.
- **Alternate:** In addition to taking over for the Seer when she is eliminated,
  the Apprentice Seer targets a player every night, but only learns if they are a
  Werewolf after choosing them twice. This requires extra bookkeeping by the
  Moderator, but gives the Apprentice Seer a way to contribute without the Seer
  having to be eliminated first.

## Aura Seer
The Aura Seer looks for players with special roles that are not plain Villagers
or Werewolves. The more special roles in the game, the more powerful this role
becomes. Be sure to have at least one "evil" special role (like the Sorceress),
so the Aura Seer isn't just functioning as a village team-finder.
- **Alternate:** The Aura Seer can be used to learn if a player is on the
  villager team. This should only be used if there are a large number of
  non-Villager roles (at least 3 in addition to the regular Werewolves). The
  Cursed, Doppelganger, and Drunk appear to be on their current team.

## Bodyguard
The Bodyguard chooses a different player each night to protect (but not the same
player twice in a row). That player cannot be eliminated that night. He may not
choose himself.
- **Alternate:** The Bodyguard may not protect the same player twice in the same
  game.
- **2nd Alternate:** The Bodyguard's protected target can't be eliminated the
  next day.

## Cupid (Soulmates / Sweethearts)
Cupid selects two players on the first night; those two players become Soulmates
(Sweethearts). If both Sweethearts are on the villager team, they win with the
villager team. If they are both on the werewolf team, they win with the werewolf
team. If they are on different teams, they become their own team and only win if
they are the last two players in the game. Being a Sweetheart overrides other
winning conditions, such as those of the Lone Wolf, Tanner, and Hoodlum. The
Soulmates may have any other role in addition to being a Soulmate.
- **Alternate:** Include Cupid as an extra card when dealing out roles. After
  Cupid selects his targets, he receives the leftover card and becomes that role
  for the rest of the game.
- **2nd Alternate:** The Moderator randomly (or intentionally) chooses the
  Sweethearts, so no one knows who the Sweethearts are except themselves.

## Cult Leader
The Cult Leader picks a player each night to add to the cult (players picked do
not know they are in the cult). The Cult Leader only wins if all players left in
the game (not necessarily including himself) are part of the cult. Normal
victory conditions for the other teams are still present. The Moderator will need
to keep a running list of cult members.
- **Alternate:** If the Cult Leader is eliminated, the first cult member he
  picked becomes the new Cult Leader (the Moderator taps this player on the
  shoulder when calling for the Cult Leader). The new Cult Leader continues to
  add members to the cult at night, in addition to any other role they might
  have. The new Cult Leader joins any previous Cult Leaders on the Cult team,
  and will only win if the only players left in the game at the end are in the
  cult.

## Cursed
The Cursed is on the Village team, unless he is targeted by the Werewolves —
then he joins the werewolf team instead of being eliminated. Each night, the
Moderator should wake the Cursed to let him know if he has switched teams (and
once he has switched teams, the Moderator should continue to wake him so the
players do not know he has been changed).

## Diseased
If the Werewolves target and eliminate the Diseased player, they skip targeting
the following night because they get sick. If the game does not have role
reveal, the Werewolves still pick a target the following night, but that target
is not eliminated. If the game has role reveal, the Werewolves do not pick a
target.

## Doppelganger
The Doppelganger selects a player the first night. If that player is eliminated,
the Doppelganger secretly takes over that role. Until her target is eliminated,
the Doppelganger is on the Villager team. The Moderator should clearly indicate
to the Doppelganger what her new role is by secretly tapping her on the shoulder
at night as soon as she gets her new role. The Doppelganger appears as a
Villager to the Seer until her target is eliminated, then is shown as her new
role after that time. If the Doppelganger becomes a Werewolf, she joins the
werewolf team.
- **Alternate:** The Doppelganger selects a player the first night and she
  instantly becomes that role (the original holder of that role is also that
  role). The Doppelganger must be called every night when using this variant to
  allow her to do her special action if she has one (if she doesn't have a
  special role, she should still be called so the other players don't know what
  role she has). This can tilt the game viciously depending on the role obtained.

## Drunk
The Drunk thinks he is a regular Villager for the first two days, and does not
know what he really is until the third night, when the Moderator wakes him and
shows him his real role. The Drunk is seen by the Seer as his actual role (even
if he doesn't know it yet). During deck preparation, mix a Werewolf, a
Villager, and most or all of the other special roles together and pull one out
without showing it to the players. That will be the Drunk's role.

## Ghost
The Ghost is eliminated the first night. He writes down 10 letters at the end of
the first night as a message from beyond, with the only limitation being that he
may not attempt to identify any player by name or initials. Each morning, the
Moderator reveals one letter (in order) from the Ghost. The clues can be
interpreted in many ways, and make conversation during the first day much more
animated than otherwise.
- **Alternate:** The first player to be eliminated becomes the Ghost, except
  that he retains his original team. If the game is not a role reveal game, the
  players will have to guess what team the Ghost is on.

## Hoodlum
On the first night, the Hoodlum indicates two players. The only way the Hoodlum
can win is if both of those players are eliminated by the end of the game and
the Hoodlum is still in the game. Normal victory conditions for the other teams
are still present, so the Hoodlum needs the Villager team to win in order for
him to win as well.
- **Alternate:** The Hoodlum picks four players, and if the Hoodlum is still in
  the game when those players are eliminated, the game is over and the Hoodlum
  wins instantly, and all other teams (except possibly the Cult Leader) lose.

## Hunter
If the Hunter is eliminated (during the day or night), he immediately fires his
weapon by pointing at any player, who is then eliminated. He may also choose to
fire his weapon straight up, resulting in no additional elimination. No one may
speak while the Hunter chooses a target. If the Hunter is eliminated at night,
he picks his target immediately the next morning.
- **Vampire Hunter variant:** If the Hunter is eliminated by Vampires, he
  automatically eliminates the Vampire sitting closest to him.

## Lone Wolf
The Lone Wolf only wins if he is the last player standing (or achieves parity
with the village by having only one other non-Werewolf player left in the game).
The Lone Wolf wakes with the Werewolves to choose a target each night. This role
is best if added at random to the deck; players should not know for certain if
this role is included.

## Lycan
The Lycan has a dormant strain of werewolfism, and appears to be a Werewolf to
the Seer even though he's not. This role should be added at random to the deck;
players should not know for certain if this role is included.
- **Alternate:** In role reveal games, the Lycan is shown to the players to be a
  Werewolf when he is eliminated.

## Masons
The first night the Moderator calls for the Masons to open their eyes and look
for the other Mason(s). No one in the village may directly or indirectly speak
of the Masons, or the players who speak of them are eliminated that night by the
secret society in order to keep it a secret. If they are eliminated by the
secret society, they automatically lose, even if they are on a winning team. The
Moderator needs to use discretion here; it's much better for gameplay if the
subject is never discussed directly or indirectly. Players will only make this
mistake once.

## Mayor
The Mayor can be a normal role card handed to a player, or the players can vote
on a Mayor on the first day, with that Mayor being in place until he is
eliminated, at which point another Mayor must be elected.

## Minion
No card is required for the standard use of this role. The first night, the
Werewolves pick a player, and that person becomes the Werewolves' Minion and
moves to the Werewolf team. The player learns who the Werewolves are, but does
not wake with them at night. The Seer sees the Minion as a Villager. If the
Minion has another special role, that role remains intact (so it is possible to
have a Seer Minion, though that would be very bad for the village team). If
playing with both Vampires and Werewolves, each team may have a Minion.
- **Alternate:** Deal this card as a regular role, and show the Minion who the
  Werewolves are and show the Werewolves who the Minion is. The card prevents
  the Minion from having a dual role.

## Old Hag
Each night, the Old Hag places a pox on a player; that player must leave the
game area for one day, and may not take part in discussions, elimination, or any
other activities that happen during the day (that player is also safe from the
Hunter or any other method of elimination from another player). The Old Hag may
not place a pox on herself or the same player twice in one game.
- **Alternate:** After the initial target returns, that night the target chooses
  the next player to place a pox on, and so on. If a player who had the pox
  placed on them is eliminated at night, the Old Hag places a pox on another
  player the following night.

## Pacifist
The Pacifist always votes for players to stay in the game. The Pacifist is a
nice boost for the Werewolf team, as it's one extra vote to prevent players from
being eliminated during the day. This role should be added at random to the
deck; players should not know for certain if this role is included.

## Paranormal Investigator (P.I.)
One night during the game, the Paranormal Investigator indicates one player, and
is told if at least one of the players to the target's immediate left or right,
or the target himself, is a Werewolf. The Moderator does not say which of those
players is a Werewolf, just that at least one of them is.

## Priest
The Priest protects a player with holy power each night. The next attempt to
eliminate that player by any means fails. The Priest may not choose himself.
<!-- ponytail: main text partially garbled in source; "If the Priest is
     eliminated after..." clause is unreadable in the PDF extraction. -->
- **Alternate:** Once per game, the Priest may learn the role of an eliminated
  player. This version of the role is only used in the "No Reveal" variant.

## Prince
If the Prince is voted on to be eliminated during the day, he is revealed as the
Prince, and is not eliminated. The Village falls asleep immediately after this
failed elimination, as if it was successful.

## Seer
Each night, the Seer points at a player, and the Moderator shows her the "V"
sign for Villager and the "W" sign for Werewolf. To be extra clear that a player
is a Werewolf, the Moderator may use another indicator (such as bringing up both
hands as claws and making a "growling" face).

## Sorceress
The Sorceress looks for the Seer each night (the Moderator shows a "thumbs up"
if the Sorceress points to the Seer). The Werewolves do not know who the
Sorceress is, and the Sorceress does not know who the Werewolves are. The Seer
sees the Sorceress as a Villager.
- **Alternate:** The Sorceress is shown who the Werewolves are on the first
  night. This is a significant advantage for the werewolf team.

## Spellcaster
Each night, choose one player. If that player is a werewolf, they become cursed
and lose all werewolf abilities for the next day and night. Cursed werewolves
cannot kill, use their role powers, or benefit from abilities that require them
to be an active werewolf. The Spellcaster does not learn whether their target was
successfully cursed. If the chosen player is not a werewolf, nothing happens.

## Tanner
The Tanner only wins if he is eliminated. Victory conditions for the other
teams are still present; the game continues after the Tanner wins.
- **Alternate:** Only the Tanner wins if he is eliminated; all the other teams
  lose if the Tanner wins. This causes a great deal of angst and stress among
  the players, and is another angle to argue (until the Seer checks them, of
  course).

## Tough Guy
If targeted by the Werewolves, the Tough Guy is eliminated the following night
(instead of the same night he was targeted). The players are told that no one
has been eliminated on the night he is targeted. The following night there may
be two or more player eliminations: the Tough Guy as well as any other
characters eliminated that night. The Moderator will need to wake up the Tough
Guy on the first night so that if he is targeted by Werewolves, the situation
can be handled properly.

## Troublemaker
Once per game, the Troublemaker can call for two eliminations the following day.
Until she does this, she is woken up by the Moderator each night.

## Vampires
The Vampires are a third major team in addition to the Werewolf team and the
Village team. Vampires choose a target each night (in addition to the
Werewolves' target), but that target is not revealed until an accusation occurs;
the Vampire target is eliminated at that time. Vampires can't be eliminated by
Werewolves, making them slightly more powerful than their furry counterparts.
With three major teams, the Vampires or Werewolves need to totally eliminate the
other two teams before declaring a victory. Vampires may be used in experienced
large groups, substituting Vampires for Werewolves (the total number of
Werewolves + Vampires should be the same as the number of Werewolves in a
regular game). All special roles that target Werewolves (Seer, P.I., etc.) may
also target Vampires (at the Moderator's discretion), but do not distinguish
between the two teams.

## Village Idiot
The Village Idiot's vote is always recorded as a down vote. This role should be
added at random to the deck; players should not know for certain if this role is
included.

- **Alternate:** Do not deal this card, but instead randomly determine who the
  Village Idiot is. That player does not know he is a Village Idiot, and his
  vote is always recorded as a down vote.

## Villagers
Villagers have no special powers. Their sole purpose is to find the Werewolves
and eliminate them.

## Werewolf
The Werewolves learn the identities of the other Werewolves the first night.
Every night after the first night, the Werewolves must agree on a target to
eliminate. If the Werewolves spend more than a minute determining a target for
the night, they do not get to choose a target that night. The Werewolves may not
target another Werewolf at night. Werewolves try to keep their identity a secret
during the day.

## Witch
The Witch may use her power to prevent someone from being eliminated once during
the game, to save someone targeted at night. She may also use her other power to
eliminate a player once during the game — the player of her choice. The
Moderator should call the Witch every night and ask if she would like to use
either or both of her special powers, even if they have been used. Both powers
may be used on the same night.
- **Alternate:** The Witch may only use one of her two powers during the game,
  but may choose which one to use.

## Wolf Cub
The Wolf Cub is a Werewolf, and wakes with them each night. If the Wolf Cub is
eliminated, the Werewolves get to eliminate two players the next night.
